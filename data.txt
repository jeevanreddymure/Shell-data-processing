<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Macronutrients_that_provide_energy">Macronutrients that provide energy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_macronutrients&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Macronutrients that provide energy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are three principal classes of <a href="/wiki/Nutrient" title="Nutrient">macronutrients</a>: <a href="/wiki/Carbohydrate" title="Carbohydrate">carbohydrate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Protein_(nutrient)" title="Protein (nutrient)">protein</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lipids" class="mw-redirect" title="Lipids">lipids</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Macronutrients are defined as a class of <a href="/wiki/Chemical_compounds" class="mw-redirect" title="Chemical compounds">chemical compounds</a> which humans consume in the largest quantities (must be above a threshold amount) and which provide humans with the bulk of <a href="/wiki/Food_energy" title="Food energy">energy</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>. While water does make up a large proportion of the total mass ingested as part of a normal diet, it does not provide any nutritional value.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Macronutrients_that_do_not_provide_energy">Macronutrients that do not provide energy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_macronutrients&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Macronutrients that do not provide energy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Water">Water</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_macronutrients&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Water">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">Water</a> is the most important substance for life on Earth. It provides the medium in which all <a href="/wiki/Metabolism" title="Metabolism">metabolic processes</a> proceed. As such it is necessary for the absorption of <a href="/wiki/Macronutrients" class="mw-redirect" title="Macronutrients">macronutrients</a>, but it provides no nutritional value in and of itself. Water often contains naturally occurring <a href="/wiki/Micronutrients" class="mw-redirect" title="Micronutrients">micronutrients</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Calcium" title="Calcium">calcium</a> and salts, and others can be introduced to the water supply such as <a href="/wiki/Chlorine" title="Chlorine">chlorine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fluoride" title="Fluoride">fluoride</a> for various purposes such as sanitation or dental health.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fiber">Fiber</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_macronutrients&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Fiber">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Dietary_fiber" title="Dietary fiber">Dietary fiber</a> or roughage, found mostly in vegetables (and fruit), is crucial to the health and normal function of the <a href="/wiki/Human_digestive_system" title="Human digestive system">human digestive system</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fitness_and_bodybuilding">Fitness and bodybuilding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_macronutrients&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Fitness and bodybuilding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In sports, <a href="/wiki/Fitness_and_figure_competition" title="Fitness and figure competition">fitness</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bodybuilding" title="Bodybuilding">bodybuilding</a> the term <b>macros</b> is used extensively to refer to macronutrients (protein, carbohydrates and lipids). Additionally, the term <i>carb</i> or <i>carbs</i> refers to carbohydrates.
</p><p>Almost all diet plans are based on judicious distribution of these three elements in required ratios. For example, a 2000 calorie diet could consist of 800 calories from 200 grams of protein (1 g of protein = 4 kcal), 800 calories from 200 grams of carbs (1 g carbohydrates = 4 kcal) and 400 calories from  44 grams of fats (1 g fat = 9 kcal).
</p><p>This is also called <i>counting</i> or <i>tracking macros</i>.
</p>
